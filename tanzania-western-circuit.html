Western Circuit

Tanzania’s Untamed Jungle Frontier

Remote, wild, and deeply immersive – the Western Circuit is where chimpanzees, dense rainforest, and untouched ecosystems define the safari experience.

Ranked by remoteness & wildlife rarity

Chimpanzee Research Legacy
Best: Jun – Oct & Dec – Feb
Jane Goodall · Chimp Tracking

Gombe Stream National Park

35 km²Smallest Park
150+Chimp Population
Lake TanganyikaScenic Backdrop

Made famous by Jane Goodall’s pioneering research, Gombe is one of the world’s best places to observe wild chimpanzees up close. The forested slopes descend directly into Lake Tanganyika, creating a rare combination of primate trekking and lakeside wilderness.

Chimpanzee Olive Baboon Colobus Monkey Forest Birdlife
Best: Jun–Oct – easiest chimp tracking conditions
02
Best Chimp Trekking in Africa
Best: May – Oct
Remote · Forest & Beach Fusion

Mahale Mountains National Park

One of Africa’s most remote and beautiful parks, Mahale sits on the shores of Lake Tanganyika with forest-covered mountains rising directly from the water. It is home to one of the highest densities of wild chimpanzees in the world, accessible only by boat or charter.

Chimpanzee Red Colobus Fishing Eagles Butterfly Species
Best: May–Oct – chimpanzees easier to track in dry forest
03
Wildest Safari in Tanzania
Best: Jun – Oct
Untouched · Massive Herds

Katavi National Park

One of Africa’s least visited parks, Katavi delivers raw wilderness with massive buffalo herds, crocodile-filled rivers, and intense predator interactions. During dry season, wildlife concentrations become some of the densest in Africa.

Buffalo Lion Crocodile Hippo Leopard
Best: Jun–Oct
04
Undiscovered Wilderness
Best: Jun – Nov
Remote · Rarely Visited

Lake Rukwa Region

A vast and rarely explored ecosystem between Katavi and the Great Rift Valley. Known for seasonal wetlands, hippo populations, and large predator territories.

Hippo Lion Birdlife Wetlands
Best: Jun–Nov
