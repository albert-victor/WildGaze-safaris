9 Days Selous, Mikumi & Ruaha Safari

Embark on an extraordinary nine-day safari through the untamed wilderness of Southern Tanzania. Explore the wildlife-rich landscapes of Selous (Nyerere National Park), discover the open plains and abundant game of Mikumi National Park, and experience the rugged beauty of Ruaha National Park. From river safaris and exciting game drives to encounters with elephants, lions, buffaloes, giraffes, and countless other species, this journey offers a remarkable blend of adventure, scenery, and authentic African wildlife experiences.

Southern Circuit

9 Days — Selous, Mikumi & Ruaha

The definitive southern Tanzania expedition — nine days across three of the continent's greatest wilderness areas, combining a Rufiji River boat safari in Selous, sweeping open plains in Mikumi, and the remote elephant and lion country of Ruaha National Park.

Big Five + Wild Dogs
9 Days / 8 Nights
3 Parks
1 – 12 Persons

This nine-day expedition is the most comprehensive safari available on Tanzania's southern circuit — a journey through three completely distinct ecosystems with a single expert driver-guide from start to finish. Beginning in Dar es Salaam, you travel south to the vast Selous Game Reserve, west to the accessible plains of Mikumi National Park, then deep inland to the remote river valleys of Ruaha National Park, before returning to Dar es Salaam on the final day. Three parks, three habitats, and a wildlife diversity that rivals anything the northern circuit can offer — at a fraction of the visitor numbers.

Selous opens the safari with two days of immersive game viewing, including a boat safari along the Rufiji River — one of East Africa's most intimate wildlife experiences. The reserve holds the continent's greatest lion population, significant elephant herds, and is one of the last strongholds of the African wild dog. On the Rufiji, enormous crocodiles and pods of hippopotamus surface alongside the boat while fish eagles call overhead. Mikumi follows: Tanzania's most accessible park, with open Brachystegia woodland and flood plains that concentrate zebra, wildebeest, buffalo, elephant, and giraffe in large, easily-observed groups. The park's position along the Tanzam highway makes it a natural midpoint stop, and its wildlife density during the dry season is exceptional.

Ruaha crowns the expedition with three full days in Tanzania's largest and most dramatic national park. The Great Ruaha River draws enormous elephant gatherings — sometimes hundreds of animals at a crossing — and the park's established lion prides are among the largest in Africa. A guided walking safari on Day 7 brings an entirely different dimension to the experience: on foot in the Miombo woodland with an armed ranger, the landscape slows down and every track, sound, and scent becomes part of the story. This is a safari built for those who want to go deep into the southern wilderness and come back with the kind of wildlife memories that only nine unhurried days can produce.

D1
Dar es Salaam → Selous Game Reserve
Pick-up after breakfast · ~218 km / 6 hrs · Arrive for lunch · Evening boat safari

After breakfast at your hotel in Dar es Salaam, your driver-guide meets you for a pre-departure briefing covering Selous's extraordinary scale, ecology, and what to expect during the days ahead. Depart with packed picnic lunch boxes and head south-west through the coastal lowlands, crossing the Rufiji River catchment as the landscape gradually opens into the dry acacia and Miombo woodland of the reserve. Arrive at Hippo Camp in time for a hot lunch, then rest through the heat of the afternoon before the safari's first thrilling encounter: an evening boat safari along the Rufiji River. Hippos surface alongside the boat, enormous Nile crocodiles bask on sandy banks, and fish eagles call from the tall fig trees. This is one of East Africa's most intimate wildlife experiences — extraordinary, quiet, and unlike anything delivered from a vehicle. Return to Hippo Camp for dinner and overnight.

D2
Full Day Game Drive — Selous Game Reserve
Depart 7:00am · Full day circuits · Bush picnic lunch · Overnight Hippo Camp

An early breakfast at first light and departure at 7:00am with packed lunch boxes for a full day exploring Selous's vast diversity on land. The reserve spans more than 50,000 square kilometres — larger than Switzerland — and holds the continent's greatest concentration of lions, with large prides roaming the open plains and woodland margins around the lakes and flood plains. This day covers the major wildlife circuits: the Beho Beho area, the lake system, and the Rufiji flood plains, where enormous elephant herds gather at permanent water and buffalo congregate in their hundreds. Selous is one of the last true strongholds of the African wild dog, and sightings on the reserve's open terrain are among the most prized in all of East African wildlife. With over 440 recorded bird species, the reserve is also outstanding for birding at any time of year. Return to Hippo Camp for dinner and a second overnight.

Full Day Game Drive Lion Prides African Wild Dogs Buffalo & Elephant 440+ Bird Species Bush Picnic Lunch
D3
Selous → Mikumi National Park
Depart after breakfast · ~5 hrs · Afternoon game drive on arrival · Tanswiss Lodge

After breakfast at Hippo Camp, depart with packed lunch boxes for the drive north-west to Mikumi National Park — approximately five hours through wild countryside that transitions from the Selous lowlands into the higher Uluguru Mountain foothills before descending onto Mikumi's broad Mkata flood plains. Arrive in the late afternoon and enter the park directly for a game drive that introduces Mikumi's signature open landscape and extraordinary plains game density. Zebra, wildebeest, impala, warthog, hartebeest, and eland antelope move freely across the wide flood plains; giraffe and buffalo are regular sightings in the woodland margins; elephants and hippos frequent the pools and luggas year-round. Check in at Tanswiss Lodge for dinner and overnight at the park boundary.

Transit to Mikumi Mkata Flood Plains Zebra & Wildebeest Giraffe & Buffalo Tanswiss Lodge Overnight
D4
Full Day Game Drive — Mikumi National Park
Depart 7:00am · Full day circuits · Picnic lunch at midday · Overnight Tanswiss Lodge

A full day inside Mikumi with packed lunch boxes for extended circuits of the park's best game-viewing areas. The Mkata flood plain at the park's heart is one of the most productive game-viewing environments in Tanzania during the dry season — water concentrates enormous numbers of animals into predictable patterns, and lion sightings on the open plains are reliable and often extended. The Hippo Pools at the Mkata River are a particular highlight: a congregation of hippos visible from a fixed viewpoint, surrounded by bird activity and the chance of leopard in the riverine fringing forest nearby. The park's Brachystegia woodland on the northern and eastern boundaries adds a different character — quieter, shadier, and excellent for species like greater kudu, eland, and sable antelope. Return to Tanswiss Lodge for dinner and a second overnight in Mikumi.

Mkata Flood Plains Hippo Pools Lion Sightings Leopard Country Sable & Eland Picnic Lunch
D5
Mikumi → Iringa → Ruaha National Park
Depart early · Via Iringa · Chief Mkwawa Museum · Arrive Ruaha · Hilltop Lodge

Depart Mikumi after an early breakfast with packed lunch boxes for the drive west to Ruaha — approximately 274 km through Tanzania's beautiful highland interior via Iringa town. The route climbs through coffee estates, granite hills, and fertile valley farmland before reaching Iringa, where a brief stop at the Chief Mkwawa Museum pays tribute to one of East Africa's most celebrated resistance leaders. Chief Mkwawa led the Hehe people's legendary resistance against German colonial forces in the 1890s — his story is a remarkable chapter in Tanzanian history, and the museum that holds his remains is a moving stop on any journey through this region. Continue west from Iringa descending into the Great Ruaha River valley — the dramatic shift in landscape signals the beginning of Ruaha's remote wilderness world. Check in at Hilltop Lodge for dinner and overnight, with three full days of game driving ahead.

Highland Drive Iringa Town Chief Mkwawa Museum Enter Ruaha Hilltop Lodge Overnight
D6
Full Day Game Drive — Ruaha National Park
Depart 7:00am · River circuits · Packed lunch · Overnight Hilltop Lodge

The first full day in Ruaha begins before sunrise with an early breakfast and a 7:00am departure into the park with packed lunch boxes. Ruaha is Tanzania's largest national park — a sweeping Miombo woodland landscape cut by the Great Ruaha River, whose permanent water draws spectacular concentrations of wildlife during the dry season. The first circuits explore the river valley, where elephant herds of 50 to 200 animals gather at crossing points, lions patrol the banks, and hippos cluster in the deeper pools. Ruaha's elephant population is believed to be the greatest concentration of any national park in East Africa, and close-range sightings from the vehicle are the norm rather than the exception. The dry-country antelope specialists that define Ruaha — greater and lesser kudu, sable and roan antelope, fringe-eared oryx — appear along the woodland margins. With 571 recorded bird species, Ruaha is also Tanzania's finest birding destination. Return to Hilltop Lodge for dinner and overnight.

D7
Game Drive + Guided Walking Safari — Ruaha
AM game drive · Picnic lunch · 3-hr afternoon walking safari · Overnight Hilltop Lodge

A second full day in Ruaha structured around the expedition's most distinctive experience: a three-hour guided walking safari in the Miombo bush. The morning begins with an early game drive across the river circuits and woodland areas, covering new ground from the previous day and seeking out African wild dogs — regularly tracked through the park and one of the most coveted sightings in all of East African safari wildlife. After a midday picnic lunch at the vehicle, the afternoon shifts entirely. On foot with your driver-guide and an armed ranger, the scale and character of the bush changes completely — what was landscape from a vehicle becomes immediate, textured, and alive. Tracks in the sand, the spoor of elephant at a waterhole, dung beetle at work, a ground hornbill calling across the Miombo. The three-hour walk covers terrain typically inaccessible by vehicle and offers a genuinely intimate encounter with Ruaha's wilderness. Return to Hilltop Lodge for dinner and overnight.

AM Game Drive African Wild Dogs Guided Walking Safari Armed Ranger Miombo Woodland
D8
Full Day — Final Ruaha Game Drives
Pre-dawn depart · Three-drive day · Sunset from the bush · Final overnight lodge

The safari's final full day in the field — and a day structured to make the most of every hour of light in one of Africa's most extraordinary parks. Depart before dawn for a sunrise game drive, when nocturnal predators are still active and the golden light transforms Ruaha's Miombo woodland into something otherworldly. Leopards in the riverine woodland, lion prides finishing their night's hunting, and enormous elephant herds moving to morning shade are all realistic at first light. Return to the lodge for a mid-morning rest and late breakfast before heading out again in the early afternoon — the hottest part of the day brings dramatic scenes at waterholes and river crossings, as animals are forced to drink regardless of temperature. The final drive of the expedition closes at sunset, watching the Miombo silhouette against a deep red sky — among the most beautiful evenings in East Africa. Return to the lodge for dinner at leisure and a final overnight in Ruaha's wilderness.

Sunrise Drive Nocturnal Predators Waterhole Scenes Sunset From the Bush Three-Drive Day
D9
Ruaha → Dar es Salaam (Return)
Breakfast · Packed lunch · Exit game drive · ~8 hrs · Evening arrival Dar es Salaam

A relaxed final breakfast at the lodge before an 8:30am departure with packed lunch boxes for the long return drive to Dar es Salaam. The exit route through Ruaha is a game drive in itself — the morning hours are consistently productive, and the road to the park gate frequently delivers final memorable sightings: crocodiles on the river banks, monitor lizards on sun-warmed rocks, agama lizards scaling the kopjes, and the possibility of a last lion encounter in the early light. Depart through the gate and begin the journey east — approximately eight hours of driving via Iringa and Mikumi before reaching the coast. Your driver-guide delivers you to your hotel or Julius Nyerere International Airport in the evening, comfortably in time for any onward connections. Nine days, three of Africa's greatest parks, and a southern circuit expedition that leaves the crowds of the northern safari circuit far behind.

Travel Information

Getting to Dar es Salaam

This tour starts and ends in Dar es Salaam. Our team handles all ground logistics from airport pick-up on Day 1 to final drop-off on Day 9.

1
This tour starts and ends in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania. Wild Gaze Safaris is also based in Iringa — the central gateway town on the southern circuit route — and our team operates across all three parks on this itinerary.
2
Fly to/from Julius Nyerere International Airport, Dar es Salaam (DAR) — Tanzania's principal international hub with direct connections from Europe, the Middle East, and Asia. Alternatively, fly into Nduli Airport, Iringa (IRI) via Dar es Salaam on domestic connections if you prefer to join the tour from Iringa on Day 5.
3
Airport and hotel transfers in Dar es Salaam can be arranged by Wild Gaze Safaris at an additional cost. Please provide your flight details at least 48 hours before departure. Our drivers meet you with a name board at arrivals.
4
Additional accommodation in Dar es Salaam before or after the safari can be arranged at a range of price points. We can also help plan onward travel to Zanzibar, the Kilimanjaro region, or the northern circuit at the end of the tour.
