4 Days Camping Safari — Serengeti & Ngorongoro

A compact but powerful introduction to Tanzania's two most iconic parks — the boundless Serengeti plains and the ancient Ngorongoro Crater, with a strong chance of seeing all Big Five.

All Big Five
4 Days / 3 Nights
Camping Safari
1 – 12 Persons

This four-day safari is one of our most popular northern circuit itineraries — combining three of Tanzania's crown jewel parks in one efficient, action-packed journey. The tour takes you to Tarangire National Park or Lake Manyara (whichever offers the best wildlife viewing at the time of your visit), then on to the Serengeti, and finally into the Ngorongoro Crater for a full-day descent.

The Serengeti needs no introduction — its vast, open plains host the greatest concentration of wildlife on Earth and the spectacle of the annual Great Wildebeest Migration, where over 1.5 million animals move in a continuous circuit across the ecosystem. The Ngorongoro Crater is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, a self-contained world 600 metres below the crater rim where lions, elephants, black rhino, buffalo, and leopard all roam in a single breathtaking bowl.

All camping equipment, meals, professional driver-guide services, and park fees are fully included. Our guides are fluent in English, highly experienced in animal behaviour and bird identification, and are passionate about delivering exceptional wildlife encounters at every game drive.

D1
Arusha → Tarangire National Park
Depart Arusha 8:30am · ~3 hrs drive · Full-day game drive

Depart your hotel in Arusha at 8:30am, heading south towards Tarangire National Park — one of Tanzania's most underrated wildlife destinations and a superb choice depending on the season. Tarangire is famous for its extraordinary elephant concentrations — during the dry season, hundreds of elephants converge on the Tarangire River, the only permanent water source in the park. Ancient baobab trees dot the landscape, providing a striking backdrop for game drives. Enjoy a full day inside the park before a 45-minute drive to your overnight camp for dinner and rest.

Tarangire River Elephant Herds Ancient Baobabs Camp Dinner & Overnight
D2
Tarangire → Serengeti National Park
Depart after breakfast · Via Ngorongoro Conservation Area · ~5 hrs driving

After breakfast, depart with your packed lunch box towards the Serengeti, travelling the scenic route through the Ngorongoro Conservation Area. This transit itself is part of the safari experience — the road climbs to the crater rim at over 2,400 metres before descending through the highland forest. Baboon troops are a near-certainty at the Ngorongoro gate area. You will stop here and there for opportunistic wildlife sightings en route — this is on-route game driving at its most rewarding. The vast plains of the Serengeti open before you approximately three hours after crossing the conservation area. Arrive at camp for dinner and overnight under an incomparable sky.

En-Route Game Drive Ngorongoro Highlands Baboon Territory Serengeti Arrival
D3
Serengeti → Ngorongoro Conservation Area
Morning game drive · Early hot lunch · Depart ~1pm · Simba Camp by 5pm

Rise before dawn for a sunrise game drive across the Serengeti — often the most rewarding hours of the day, when predators are still active and the golden light transforms the plains. After a hot early lunch at camp, you begin the drive back towards Ngorongoro Conservation Area. En route, you have the option of visiting a traditional Maasai village for a cultural excursion — a rare opportunity to engage with one of East Africa's most iconic peoples (available at additional cost). Arrive at the Simba Campsite on the crater rim by 5pm for dinner and overnight, sleeping at 2,400 metres with the vast Ngorongoro Crater spread below you in the failing light.

Sunrise Game Drive Optional Maasai Village Simba Camp · Crater Rim 2,400m Overnight
D4
Ngorongoro Crater → Arusha
Crater descent at dawn · Game drive to midday · Drive to Arusha by evening

After a very early breakfast, begin the steep descent into the Ngorongoro Crater floor — a World Heritage Site containing one of Africa's densest wildlife populations within its 260 square kilometre bowl. The crater holds an estimated 25,000 large animals, including the critically endangered black rhino, enormous lion prides, huge herds of wildebeest and zebra, spotted hyena, hippos in the crater lakes, and occasional leopard sightings. Enjoy game drives through the crater until midday lunch, before making the ascent back to the rim and returning to Arusha by early evening — in time for your overnight stay or onward connection to Zanzibar, Kilimanjaro, or your home destination.

Travel Information

Getting to Arusha

This tour starts and ends in Arusha. Our team handles all ground logistics from the moment you land at the airport.

1
This tour starts and ends in Arusha, Tanzania. The most convenient airport is Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO), located approximately 40 km east of Arusha on the road to Moshi. Most international travellers transit through Nairobi (NBO), Dar es Salaam (DAR), or Addis Ababa (ADD).
2
Fly to/from Kilimanjaro Airport (JRO) or Arusha Airport (ARK) via Dar es Salaam or Nairobi. Direct flights from Europe are available on KLM (Amsterdam) and several charter carriers. Check current prices and availability through your preferred booking platform.
3
Airport transfers to and from Arusha can be arranged by Wild Gaze Safaris at an additional cost. Please provide your flight details at least 48 hours in advance. Our drivers meet you in the arrivals hall with a name board.
4
Additional accommodation in Arusha before or after the safari can be arranged at a range of price points — from comfortable guesthouses to boutique lodges. We can also arrange onward connections to Zanzibar or the coast at the end of the tour.
